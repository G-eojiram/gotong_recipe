What is Sour Soup?

 Filipino sour soup, famously known as "sinigang," is a culinary masterpiece celebrated for its distinctive tangy flavor. This comforting soup is characterized by its sour taste, achieved through the use of tamarind as a key ingredient.

Sinigang typically features a variety of meats, such as pork, beef, shrimp, or fish, harmoniously simmered with an array of vegetables like water spinach, radish, eggplant, and string beans. Seasoned with fish sauce or shrimp paste, sinigang captures the essence of Filipino cuisine with its perfect balance of savory and sour notes, providing a comforting and flavorful experience in every bowl.

Pork Sinigang

Pork Sinigang, an iconic Filipino dish, is a tantalizing sour soup that marries succulent pork with an array of fresh vegetables.

The distinctive tangy flavor, derived from tamarind, defines this beloved dish. With its harmonious blend of savory and sour notes, Pork Sinigang stands as a comforting and flavorful representation of Filipino culinary mastery.
