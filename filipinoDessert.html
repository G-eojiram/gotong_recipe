How to Cook Filipino Dessert?

What is Filipino Dessert?

  Filipino desserts, referred to as "panghimagas" or "himagas," embody a delightful spectrum of sweet treats that beautifully conclude a Filipino meal. These desserts feature a rich variety of flavors and textures, often incorporating local ingredients such as coconut, rice, sweet beans, and tropical fruits.

From the refreshing shaved ice delight of halo-halo to the decadent creaminess of leche flan, Filipino desserts showcase a balance of sweetness and creativity. Whether enjoyed as a snack or a post-meal indulgence, these treats are a celebration of Filipino hospitality and the country's diverse culinary heritage.

How to Cook Biko?

Biko

Biko, a cherished Filipino dessert, is a sweet and sticky rice cake that captivates taste buds with its rich and caramelized flavors. Crafted from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and brown sugar, Biko is a delectable treat often enjoyed during festive occasions. Its irresistible chewy texture and sweet coconut undertones make Biko a comforting and iconic delight in Filipino cuisine.
