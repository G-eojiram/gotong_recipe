How to Cook Pork Adobo?

What is Biko?

 Biko is a delicious treat that Filipinos all across the world enjoy. Biko is a type of sticky rice cake, otherwise known as kakanin. With a combination of coconut milk and brown sugar, biko is a delicious dessert or merienda to share with your loved ones! You can often find biko at birthday parties, fiestas, holiday parties, and family reunions, usually with other sticky rice treats.

Biko Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice (aka sticky rice or malagkit)
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Banana leaves (for lining the pan, optional)
  • Grated coconut for topping (optional)

Procedures / Steps

Step 1: Soaking and Cooking Rice

  • Wash the glutinous rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  • Drain the rice and place it in a wide, heavy-bottomed pan.

Step 2: Adding Coconut Milk

  • Pour coconut milk over the rice and stir to combine.
  • Cook the rice and coconut milk mixture over medium heat, stirring continuously to prevent sticking, until the rice is partially cooked.

Step 3: Adding Sugar and Salt

  • Add brown sugar and salt to the partially cooked rice.
  • Continue cooking and stirring until the mixture thickens and becomes sticky.

Step 4: Preparing the Pan

  • Line a baking dish or pan with banana leaves if available. This is optional but adds a traditional touch to the biko.

Step 5: Transferring the Mixture

  • Transfer the cooked rice mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it evenly.

Step 6. Baking or Steaming Process

  • Bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (180°C) for about 20-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown. Alternatively, you can steam the biko until set.

Note: If you don’t have an oven, you can use a Stove. Follow this procedure:

  • Lower the heat to medium-low and continue cooking the rice mixture in the pan.
  • Stir continuously to prevent burning and to ensure even cooking.
  • Cook the mixture until it thickens, becomes sticky, and turns a golden brown color. This may take about 20-30 minutes.

Step 7: Cooling and Serving

  • Allow the biko to cool before slicing into squares or diamond shapes.
  • Optional: Top with grated coconut for added flavor.

Step 8: Prepare for Serving

  • Serve the biko squares on banana leaves or your preferred serving dish.
