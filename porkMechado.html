What is Pork Mechado?

 

 Pork Mechado is a Filipino pork stew. This particular recipe is composed of pork slices, potato, frozen green peas, and bell peppers. A hearty Filipino stew made with tender chunks of pork, often from the shoulder or belly, cooked in a savory tomato-based sauce. It is typically seasoned with soy sauce, vinegar, and a mix of aromatic spices. This dish showcases the delicious blend of sweet and savory flavors, making it a comforting and well-loved Filipino favorite. It is a simple dish that can easily be prepared by anyone.

Pork Mechado Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into cubes
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup beef or pork broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 large potato, peeled and sliced into rounds
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and sliced into rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil

Procedures / Steps

Step 1: Marinate the Pork

The first thing to do when making Pork Mechado is to marinate the pork in at least 10 minutes.

  • Combine pork, soy sauce, and juice of 1 lime in a bowl. Mix well and let it stay for 10 minutes.

Note: Calamansi (or calamondin) can be used as a substitute for lime. This helps provide flavor to the pork and enhances the overall taste of the dish.

Step 2: Prepare for Cooking or Saut√©ing

  • Heat the oil in a cooking pot.
  • Saute the garlic and onion until the onion gets soft.

Step 3: Browning the Marinated Pork

  • Add the marinated pork (include the remaining marinade). Cook until it pork turns light brown.

Step 4: Add the Spices or Liquid Ingredients and Seasonings

  • Pour tomato sauce and water. Let boil.
  • Add the Knorr Pork Cube. Stir. Cover and cook in low heat for 35 to 40 minutes.
  • Add the potato and green peas. Cook for 10 minutes.
  • Stir-in the bell peppers. Continue to cook for 5 minutes.
  • Add salt and ground black pepper to taste.

Cook the pork until tender. Do this by pouring the remaining marinade, if any. Also add water. Let the liquid boil.

Step 5: Prepare for Serving

  • Transfer to a serving plate and Serve the Pork Mechado.
Note:
  • Pork Mechado is often garnished with green peas for added color and flavor.
  • Adjust the thickness of the sauce by simmering longer for a thicker consistency.
  • This Mechado recipe can also be made with beef, using similar steps but adjusting the cooking time for the beef's tenderness.
