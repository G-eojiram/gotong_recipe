How to Cook Pork Adobo?

What is Pork Adobo?

 Pork Adobo is a classic Filipino dish known for its savory and slightly tangy flavor. It involves marinating pork pieces in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices, then braising or simmering until the meat becomes tender. It's a beloved dish that showcases the balance of sweet, salty, and sour flavors.

Pork Adobo Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup vinegar (cane or rice vinegar)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 head of garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • 2 to 3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • 1 cup water
  • Cooking oil (for sautéing)

Procedures / Steps

Step 1: Marinate the Pork Belly in Soy Sauce and Crushed Garlic

The first thing to do is marinate the pork belly in soy sauce and crushed garlic. It is best to marinate it overnight.
If time is limited, one hour should be enough. Some like to add vinegar during the process. You may do so if preferred.

  • In a bowl, combine pork pieces, soy sauce, minced garlic, whole peppercorns, and bay leaves.
  • Massage the marinade into the pork, ensuring even coating.
  • Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate.

Step 2: Prepare for Cooking or Sautéing

  • Heat a cooking pot or a deep pan over medium heat.
  • Add cooking oil, then sauté sliced onions until translucent.

Step 3: Browning the Marinated Pork

  • Drain the marinate pork.
  • Add the marinated pork to the pot.
  • Brown the pork on all sides, enhancing the flavor.

Step 4: Add the Spices or Liquid Ingredients

  • Pour in the vinegar and let it simmer for a few minutes to cook off its acidity.
  • Add soy sauce and sugar (if using).
  • Mix well and let it simmer for another 5 minutes.

Cook the pork until tender. Do this by pouring the remaining marinade, if any. Also add water. Let the liquid boil.

Step 5: Pour water and Simmer

  • Pour in water to the pot, and stir to combine.
  • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  • Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 40-50 minutes or until the pork is tender.

Step 6: Adjusting Seasoning

  • Taste the adobo and adjust the seasoning according to your preference. You can add more soy sauce or vinegar if needed.

Since we already added a good amount of soy sauce, salt might not be needed. However, this depends on your preference. You can also add more water here if you think that then saltiness is on the upper side. Sometimes I also add a dash of sugar to balance the flavor.

Step 7: Finishing Touch

  • Once the pork is tender, let the adobo sauce reduce until it reaches your desired consistency.

Step 8: Prepare for Serving

  • Serve the Pork Adobo hot with steamed rice.
Note:

Pork Adobo is often enjoyed with a side of steamed white rice. Some people also like to pair it with a boiled egg or slices of ripe banana. This recipe provides a basic guide to making Filipino Pork Adobo, but variations can exist based on regional preferences and personal tastes.

Enjoy your cooking!
